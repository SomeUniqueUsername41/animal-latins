Animal Latin translator

Avoid inputting stammers and stutters (like "h-hello" for "hello"), just input the normal word and modify the stammer or stutter for it accordingly (so "ellohay", "hello" in Pig Latin, stammered/stuttered would be "e-ellohay"). If emphasizing words by putting them in all-caps, instead, put hyphens around them. Like, instead of inputting "NOT" to emphasize "not", input "-not-" to emphasize "not".


When I was around 13 or 14 years old, there was an app for a translator for Duck Latin that was just like Pig Latin, but it used the suffix "-ack" (like "quack") instead of "-ay" like in Pig Latin (the Duck Latin translator app is still on the App Store to this day). This translator takes that same idea for different animal-based Latins and uses suffixes based on qualities of, the sounds of, and things associated with the respective animals that the different animal Latins are based off of. So, for instance, Mouse Latin uses the suffix "-eak" as in "squeak", Squirrel Latin uses "-ut" as in "nut", and Camel Latin uses "-ump" as in "hump".
